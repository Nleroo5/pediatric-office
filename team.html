Trudy English ARNP, Dr. Austin Dupont, Dr. Patricia Elvir, Dr. Lindsay Wolf

Dr. Austin Dupont, MD

Owner & Lead Pediatrician

University of Toledo

Dr. Austin Dupont completed medical school and pediatric residency in Toledo, Ohio, before moving to Jacksonville with his wife, Jade, in 2022. After an unforgettable honeymoon backpacking through Europe, they welcomed their daughter, Daisy Mae, in fall 2024.

In September 2025, he purchased Village Pediatrics from Dr. Pat Elvir, carrying on her legacy of warm, family-centered care. Known for being easy-going and fun (expect a high-five or fist bump!), he takes your child's health very seriously.

Personal Life

Enjoys church, family time, woodworking, archery with his compound bow, and traveling. Fun fact: handled many office renovations himself, including adding a 4th exam room!

Special Interests

  • •ADHD Diagnosis & Management
  • •Anxiety & Depression
  • •Asthma Management
  • •Constipation Management
  • •Healthy Habits Program

Dr. Patricia Elvir, MD

FAAP, ABIHM

Founding Physician

Native New Yorker who did her pediatric residency at Schneider Children's Hospital of Long Island Jewish Medical Center, serving as Chief Resident. Attended medical school at University of San Carlos in Guatemala City, Guatemala.

Dual board certified with American Board of Pediatrics and American Board of Integrative and Holistic Medicine, plus Fellow of American Board of Pediatrics. Founded Village Pediatrics in St. Augustine in summer 2007.

Ministry & Mission Work

Ordained minister with The Apostolic Network of Global Awakening. Founded "Amigos de Juanita" for medical missions to Central America. Has served in Guatemala, Nicaragua, and Haiti.

Background

  • •Fluent in Spanish
  • •20+ years clinical & academic experience
  • •Relocated from NY to Jacksonville 2005
  • •Mother of two sons

Trudy English, ARNP

Pediatric Nurse Practitioner

University of Akron BSN, Ohio State MSN

Graduated from University of Akron with BSN in 1989. After a short time at Duke University working with autologous bone marrow transplant patients, she found her passion for pediatrics and spent years in the NICU at Ohio State University Medical Center.

Graduated with Masters in advanced pediatric nursing from Ohio State in 1996, becoming certified as a pediatric nurse practitioner the same year. Began career in primary care pediatrics in Columbus, then Jacksonville. With Village Pediatrics since 2015.

Family & Interests

Married to Robert with three children (twins Grace and Will, daughter Annie). Enjoys caring for barn animals (2 donkeys, 3 goats, 18 hens!), traveling, beach time, puzzles, and reading.

Clinical Experience

  • •NICU specialist background
  • •Primary care pediatrics expert
  • •30+ years nursing experience
  • •Certified pediatric nurse practitioner

Dr. Lindsay Wolf, DNP

MBA, APRN, CPNP-PC, CNE, CLC

Associate Professor & Associate Dean

Full-time Associate Professor of Nursing at Jacksonville University Keigwin School of Nursing and Associate Dean of Nursing. Teaches in all programs including Traditional BSN, ABSN 16-month and 12-month programs, Graduate NP programs and Graduate non-NP programs.

Practices as autonomous pediatric nurse practitioner one day per week. Received BSN at University of North Florida, DNP and PNP degree from University of Florida, and MBA from Jacksonville University.

Certifications & Education

Certified Nurse Educator (NLN), Certified Pediatric Nurse Practitioner (PNCB), Certified Lactation Counselor (ALPP). BSN UNF, DNP/PNP UF, MBA JU.

Focus & Family

  • •Growth & development in pediatrics
  • •Children with special needs
  • •Lactation support & education
  • •Active in Catholic Church, mother of 5 daughters

Our Collaborative Approach

How our team works together to provide exceptional, coordinated care for your family

Team Collaboration

Our providers work seamlessly together, sharing expertise and ensuring coordinated care for every patient. Regular team meetings ensure everyone is aligned on your child's care plan.

Evidence-Based Care

We stay current with the latest research and medical advances, ensuring your child receives the most effective treatments backed by scientific evidence and best practices.

Family-Centered Care
